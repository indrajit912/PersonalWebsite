Dates: January 29-31, 2025

About the Symposium

The Annual Symposium organized by the Stat-Math Unit of the Indian Statistical Institute, Bangalore, provides a vibrant platform for academic exchange and collaboration. This event brings together Postdoctoral Fellows (PDFs) and Research Scholars (RSs) to present their ongoing research to an audience comprising professors, fellow researchers, and students.
This year, the symposium will span three days, with each speaker allotted a maximum of 45 minutes to share their work. The event also offers a valuable opportunity for Junior Research Fellows (JRFs) in the early stages of their academic journey to showcase their research in a supportive and intellectually stimulating environment.

Abstract. Let \(f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}\) be a smooth function. The Taylor expansion of \(f\) around a point \(x_0\) is given by \[ f(x) = f(x_0) + \nabla f(x_0) \cdot (x - x_0) + \mathcal{O}(\|x - x_0\|^2), \] where \(\nabla f(x_0)\) is the gradient of \(f\) at \(x_0\). This expansion provides an approximation of \(f\) near \(x_0\) by its linear part. The concept of a gradient leads to the definition of directional derivatives.
